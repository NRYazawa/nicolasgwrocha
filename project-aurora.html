Project Overview

Aurora is an intense survival game blending extraction shooter mechanics with psychological horror elements. You take on the role of Sophie, the daughter of the former leader of the Aurora community. After his passing, the heavy burden of leading and protecting the community falls directly into your hands. Set in an unforgiving and perilous world, the core loop demands that you survive, scavenge for vital resources, and ultimately earn the trust of your people.


Key Technical Implementations

1. Procedural World & Item Generation

To ensure high replayability and unpredictable encounters, I developed a robust procedural generation system. This logic dictates not only the layout of the exploration zones but also dynamically distributes loot and resources, forcing the player to adapt their strategy on every run.

2. Extraction Shooter Core Loop

The gameplay is heavily inspired by the tense, fast-paced, and visceral experience of The Last of Us Part 2, mixed with the high-stakes risk/reward systems of modern extraction shooters. Implementing this required creating tight inventory management, encumbrance mechanics, and a reliable save/state system to track the player's progress and the community's trust level.
