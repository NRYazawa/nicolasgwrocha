Project Overview

Elevator Down to Hell represents a major milestone in my journey as a developer: my very first 3D game. You enter an old, abandoned USSR bunker, only to encounter a grotesque figure with a partially destroyed face wielding a cleaver. To survive and escape back to the surface, you must navigate the claustrophobic corridors, hide, and collect three specific keys.


Key Technical Implementations

1. 3D Spatial Navigation & Level Design

Transitioning to a 3D environment required a completely new approach to player movement, colliders, and spatial pacing. The level design was carefully constructed to create a maze-like bunker that naturally builds tension while keeping the player intuitively guided toward their objectives.

2. Event-Driven Mechanics & Objectives

The core gameplay loop is built on a solid event-driven foundation. Finding and collecting the keys updates the game state, unlocking new areas and triggering specific enemy behaviors. Implementing this system taught me the fundamentals of object interaction and win/loss condition tracking in a tridimensional space.
